Echo Song

Houserules for D&D style Fate Accelerated

We are using Fate Accelerated as the basic ruleset for the campaign. With a few tweaks and additions to make it feel a bit more like proper Dungeons & Dragons.

PCs have six stress boxes, each one worth one stress.

Classes

Classes will be handled with stunts. Each Dungeons & Dragons class will be a combination of two 'standard' stunts. This is not to mean that these stunts can't be mixed and matched. Not to mention that a standard character can start with three stunts, so even a player wanting to go full 'Fighter' can pick up a stunt from another class or some standard stunt to help them better be a specific type of fighter they have in mind for their character.

Of course, these should be as flexible and open as any stunt. Perhaps a wizard takes the 'enemy specialization' fighter feat, but instead of killing it's studying different enemies? Every five enemy corpses (or living beings) studied in detail gives some kind of bonus.

Fighter:

Weapon Specialization - Someone with this stunt becomes more skilled with weapons as they use them. Every so many enemies killed with a specific type of weapon (sword, spear, long bow, etc) gives bonuses for using that weapon. Bonuses are situational, so mechanically this should encourage anyone with this stunt to carry around a diverse set of weapons to use whichever one has the best special ability in a situation.

Enemy Specialization - Someone with this stunt learns how to best fight enemies with experience. Every so many of a specific enemy (kobolds, slimes, etc) grants special bonuses for fighting that enemy in the future.

Wizard:

Magic - This stunt allows the character to use magic. See the 'Magic' section for rules. Stunt has both a connected Approach and a source of magical power included.

Spellcasting - Someone with this stunt can prepare spells, a more structured and powerful way to use magical power. A number of spells can be prepared equal to the PC's magic Approach and the number of mystic mutations they have. See the 'Magic' section for rules on prepared spells.

Cleric:

Magic - This stunt allows the character to use magic. See the 'Magic' section for rules. Stunt has both a connected Approach and a source of magical power included.

Miracles - Someone with this stunt can inflict mystic mutations on whoever they are using magic on instead of gaining one themself. It also makes magic more powerful when used on targets with mystic mutations from the same faith as the person with this stunt.

Monk:

Magic - This stunt allows the character to use magic. See the 'Magic' section for rules. Stunt has both a connected Approach and a source of magical power included.

Fighting Styles - Someone with this stunt can create and used combo attacks of several connected combat moves right after each other. Come up with three cool-sounding martial arts moves. If you do them in a row, each successful one gives the next a small bonus. Second and third ones get +1 to their rolls if the previous was successful, if all three are pulled off in a row, monk PC gains a fate point. The moves should be specific actions. Like: attack, create advantage, attack, against one target. Or: attack, attack, attack, all against three different oppoenents. Third example: defend, create advantage, attack, against one opponent in reaction to an attack (starting with the defend action). Monkey Paw Fist. Tiger Claw Strike. Lion Roar Kick. Meteor Roundhouse. Etc. Each move in a combo should be of the same 'style'. So three Monkey moves, or three Shining moves, etc. One move can be in more than one combo. So a monk could have five 'Shining Sky' moves that make up two different combos.

Magic Rules

The Magic stunt grants the PC the ability to spend stress boxes to add that amount of bonus to an action roll. The action should be something big and impressive, not just a normal non-magical event. Standard Stress or Magic Stress can be used to fuel magic. One point of stress spent equals +1 to roll.

Being able to use magic grants a separate stress track. Base magic stress is equal to the amount of mystic mutations the PC has. These stress boxes can only be used to fuel magic effects and spells. They reset at the end of a scene same as normal stress.

Mystic Mutations

The mortal form cannot channel magic safely. Anyone who uses any amount of magic is at risk of having their body & mind (& possibly soul) by the magic they use. The good news is that the more mutations a magic-user has, the more powerful magic they can channel. The bad news is that anyone with too many mystic mutations becomes a puppet of the magic with no free will and only wishes to further the goals of the magic that flows through them.

Whenever a PC uses magic (spends stress for a magic effect or casts a spell) they have to check to see if they suffer mystic mutation. This is a d8 roll with modifiers based on amount of stress spent and number of mystic mutations the PC has. So the full roll is 1d8 +stress spent total -mutations possessed). A result higher than the PCs current number of mutations means they get another one.

Magic Components

Exotic and magical materials that can be spent instead of stress to create magical effects. They are harvested from exotic flora and fauna. Most are expendable, only able to be used once. Rare exceptions can be reused over and over, permanently adding to the PCs magic stress track when worn/carried/bonded.

Spellcasting

Someone with the Spellcasting stunt can prepare spells. Preparing a spell has two primary advantages. One, can fuel the spell with any amount of stress up to maximum able to spend. So if a PC has a magic stress track of 4, each prepared spell can have a power of +4. Though each prepared spell does trigger a mystic mutation check when prepared. The other advantage is that preparing a spell charges the magic-user's body with magical energies letting them accomplishing small magical effects without spending stress. As well as drawing on the spell inside them for other things. Like preparing a fireball spell might allow the magic-user to see the world around them as heat and flammability instead of with light and sound. Cantrips and knowledge, basically.
